About Dr. Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. Born in a humble family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam went on to become one of India's most respected scientists, playing a pivotal role in India’s missile development and nuclear weapons program.

He was a recipient of several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian honor. A lifelong advocate of education, innovation, and youth development, Dr. Kalam continues to inspire generations long after his passing in 2015.

Key Achievements

  • 1969: Joined ISRO and led the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV) project
  • 1980: India’s first satellite, Rohini, was deployed into orbit under his leadership
  • 1998: Played a crucial role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • 2002–2007: Served as the 11th President of India
  • 2002: Launched PURA (Providing Urban Amenities to Rural Areas) mission
  • 2015: Passed away while giving a lecture at IIM Shillong
